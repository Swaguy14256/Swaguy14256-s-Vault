September 13, 2022 - 2 New YouTube Channels And Future Plans

I have created 2 more YouTube channels in preparation for whenever I actually start making YouTube videos. The first one (A Dead Guy) is an extras channel for content that does not belong to my main channel or the second new channel. The second channel (A Frozen Guy) is for gameplay and ROM Hack related videos. Keep in mind that I am not going to do live streams on this channel. On another note, I will never do any collaborations with any YouTube channel on any of my channels, so do not ask me to collaborate with any YouTuber; I will just put your request in the trash bin. One thing noteworthy about my channels is how much effort I put into their names, which is none at all. It was all for a garbage joke involving my Holiday names on SMW Central.

As for future plans for this site, the first thing I need to do is get a permanent domain for this site. Right now, the site uses ngrok's free plan, which does not allow for permanent domain names. As a result, everytime I have to reboot the site, the link is different. Right now, I have created a replit site that simply redirects to the site, which works for most pages, but so much for pages like the File Bin, and eventually the Games and Art Gallery. Therefore, before I make any major changes to the site, I will have to get a permanent domain so that way, I no longer have to change the link in the redirect function on the replit site.

Once I get a permanent domain, the next things I plan on doing include getting a proper IDE for developing the site, improving and fixing the structure and codebase for the site, and improving the design of the website. Right now, the entire site is completly developed using only a plain text editor, which is Notepad. While this is a method tha can be used, it is unintuitave because I cannot correct syntax errors or typos. In addition, I have to remember all of the HTML elements, CSS tags, and JavaScript commands when changing a page. With a proper IDE, I can maintain development of this site more easily, and I can make sure there are no syntax errors or typos. In addition, I can then fix the structure and codebase of the site. Right now, the structure and codebase of the site is garbage. A lot of elements are hard coded, which includes the News updates being confined to a single page, the Art Gallery, Games, and Files Bin not actually checking for the lack of items or not, and said pages' last update date being completely manual instead of automated. In addition, there are missing features of the site due to the limited use of JavaScript, including the ability to sort Games and Files by Name, Date Added, and Size. As a result, I plan on making the Version History page include separation of versions based on development build and version number, separating each update on the News page into their own page with previews on the main page of the News section, separating the Copyright And Disclaimers Page articles in the same way as the News page, implementing JavaScript into the Art Gallery, Games, and Files Bin to allow for automatic sorting and optimization, along with proper file checking and update dates. I will have to use metadata for sorting, though that is not complicated. Finally, the current design of the site has lots of room for improvement. I will redesign the site to look less basic and more appealing, but not in a way that makes it focused on mobile users and not desktop or laptop users. Also, I am aware that the site has no icon. That will be fixed when I redesign the site. With all of that out of the way, once I get to these plans, I should be able to exit the Pre-Alpha stage and get the site into the Alpha stage of development.
